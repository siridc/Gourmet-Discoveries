Halo-Halo

HISTORY

The origin of halo-halo is linked to pre-war Japanese Filipinos and the Japanese dessert kakigōri. Early versions, like "monggo con hielo" (inspired by Spanish "maíz con hielo"), featured mung beans cooked in syrup, served over crushed ice with milk and sugar. Over time, more local ingredients were added, creating the modern halo-halo. Unlike its Japanese counterpart, halo-halo places ingredients under the ice rather than on top.

Some attribute its creation to Japanese migrants in 1920s or 1930s Quiapo, Manila, near the Insular Ice Plant, which provided the city's ice. The name "halo-halo" means "mixed" in Tagalog. There's no fixed recipe, and ingredients vary by region based on available fruits and root crops.

Ingredients

Procedure

  • Step 1

    • Prepare Ingredients: Gather all sweetened fruits, beans, and other toppings.

  • Step 2

    • Layer Ingredients: In a tall glass or bowl, layer the sweetened beans, jackfruit, bananas, purple yam, and coconut strips.

  • Step 3

    • Add Shaved Ice: Top the layered ingredients with shaved ice, packing it slightly.

  • Step 4

    • Pour Milk: Drizzle evaporated milk over the shaved ice.

  • Step 5

    • Add Leche Flan: Place a slice of leche flan on top (if using).

  • Step 6

    • Top with Ice Cream: Add a scoop of ice cream on top.

  • Step 7

    • Garnish: Optionally, sprinkle with a pinch of sugar or additional toppings.

  • Step 8

    • Serve: Mix well before eating to combine flavors. Enjoy!
