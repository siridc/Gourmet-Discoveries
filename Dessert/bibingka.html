Bibingka

HISTORY

Bibingka is a traditional Filipino rice cake, part of the "kakanin" (rice-based desserts). Its taste resembles rice pudding but is sweeter. While its origins are debated, some believe it came from Indonesia or India, where a similar dessert, "bebinca" or "bibik," exists. The Portuguese version of bebinca, brought from Goa, differs from bibingka, which uses glutinous rice and coconut milk. Bibingka was historically served during Christmas after early morning church services, replacing the usual breakfast of "pan de sal." Another theory suggests that bibingka came from China, with the word "bi" meaning uncooked grain, and the Chinese may have introduced it to the Philippines.

Ingredients

Procedure

  • Step 1

    • Prep Banana Leaves: Soften leaves by passing over flame or dipping in hot water. Line baking dish or molds.

  • Step 2

    • Mix Dry Ingredients: In a bowl, combine rice flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.

  • Step 3

    • Mix Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, whisk coconut milk, water, and eggs.

  • Step 4

    • Combine Mixtures: Gradually mix wet ingredients into dry until smooth.

  • Step 5

    • Preheat Oven: Set to 350°F (175°C).

  • Step 6

    • Pour Batter: Fill lined dish/molds with batter (3/4 full).

  • Step 7

    • Add Toppings: Place salted egg slices and grated coconut on top.

  • Step 8

    • Bake: Bake for 30-40 minutes until toothpick comes out clean and serve!
