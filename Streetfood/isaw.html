Isaw

HISTORY

Originally considered a humble dish, Isaw originated in Manilla where food vendors utilized offal cuts, which were often discarded, to use every part of the animal. In some parts of the Philippines, beef intestines may be used instead of chicken or pork. Isaw shares similarities with grilled offal dishes found in other countries.

The term "isaw” cames from the word “sawsawan” or the sauce that you dip the intestines in.
