Buko Pandan Drink

HISTORY

Buko Pandan is a creamy, tropical dessert made with strips of coconut and pandan-flavored jelly. Itâ€™s refreshing and perfect for get-togethers or simply as an afternoon snack. It is said to have originated from the eastern Visayas region on the island of Bohol. Influenced by American occupation, locals tailored the idea of an American fruit salad to suit Filipino tastes, compiling buko strips and jelly along with cream.

Buko Pandan Drink

Ingredients

For Gulaman

Procedure

  • Step 1

    • Peel the coconut fruit at the top of its shape to remove the juice first.

  • Step 2

    • Pour all of the coconut juice in a big jar that can contain at least 3 liters.

  • Step 3

    • Chop the fruit into two and then scrape the flesh inside using a stainless coconut grater scraper.

  • Step 4

    • Scoop the scraped flesh using a spoon and mix it inside the jar.

  • Step 5

    • You can add white sugar to the juice if it doesn't taste sweet enough. Just dissolve the white sugar with a hot water then add the milk.
